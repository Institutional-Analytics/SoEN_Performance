School of Engineering — Grade Statistics Report

Spring 2024-2025 & Fall 2025-2026 | Prepared for the Dean of Engineering

Executive Summary

  • Two scopes analyzed: (1) SoEN courses taken by all students, and (2) SoAS courses taken specifically by SoEN students
  • SoEN Courses — Improvement: Pass rate improved from 82.6% (Spring) to 87.5% (Fall), fail rate dropped from 10.7% to 7.1%
  • SoAS Courses by SoEN Students — Alarming: Only 60.0% pass rate in Spring and 68.1% in Fall — far below the SoEN-internal performance
  • Key bottleneck courses: MATH210, MATH220, MATH225, CSCI250, PHYS220 — SoAS courses where SoEN students lose 30-45% to failure + withdrawal
  • EENG250 and EENG300 remain the highest-impact SoEN courses with persistent high fail+WD rates across both semesters
  • Graduate students consistently outperform undergraduates (93-94% pass rate vs 80-85%)
  • Campus variation: Bekaa leads in pass rates; Beirut, Tyre, and Rayak trail in both scopes

Part B: SoAS Courses Taken by SoEN Students

Grade statistics for Science courses (Math, Physics, Chemistry, CS) taken by Engineering students. These are the prerequisite/service courses that often become bottlenecks.

Critical finding: SoEN students struggle significantly in SoAS courses. Pass rates are 60.0% (Spring) and 68.1% (Fall) — roughly 15-20 percentage points below their performance in SoEN courses. Nearly 1 in 3 SoEN students fails or withdraws from their SoAS courses.
